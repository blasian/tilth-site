### Yellow leaves

Yellow leaves are the single most-Googled plant problem — and they mean at least six different things depending on which leaves are yellow, how fast it happened, and what else is going on.

**Likely causes:**

- **Nitrogen deficiency** — pale yellowing starting from the bottom leaves
- **Overwatering** — uniform yellowing, soggy soil, wilting despite the wet
- **Underwatering** — yellowing with crispy edges
- **Natural senescence** — oldest leaves yellow and drop, everything else fine
- **Disease** — early blight, septoria; usually starts with spots
- **Nutrient lockout from pH** — soil too acidic or alkaline, nutrients technically present but unavailable

**First check:** Which leaves are yellow? Bottom = nitrogen or water. Top = micronutrient or disease. Random = disease or pest.
