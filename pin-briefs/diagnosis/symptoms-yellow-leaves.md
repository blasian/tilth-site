# Why Are My Plant's Leaves Yellow?

## Headline
**Why are my leaves yellow?**

## Subhead
*The most-Googled plant problem means six different things — here's how to tell which.*

## Hero
A single yellowing leaf — preferably tomato or pepper, showing the gradient from green to yellow. Soft natural light. Could also be a multi-leaf composition showing different yellowing patterns side by side (bottom-up vs. interveinal vs. uniform), but a single leaf is cleaner at thumbnail.

## Body — Likely causes (6 items, bold + dash + short description)

- **Nitrogen deficiency** — pale yellowing starting from oldest (bottom) leaves
- **Overwatering** — uniform yellowing, soggy soil, sometimes wilting despite the wet
- **Underwatering** — yellowing with crispy, dry edges
- **Natural senescence** — only the oldest leaves; rest of plant looks fine
- **Disease** — early blight, septoria; usually starts with spots
- **pH lockout** — soil too acidic or alkaline, nutrients present but unavailable

## Callout (terracotta accent)

> First check: **which leaves**? Bottom = nitrogen or water. Top = micronutrient or virus. Random = pest or pathogen.
