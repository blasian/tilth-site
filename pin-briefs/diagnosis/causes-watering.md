# Overwatering vs. Underwatering

## Headline
**Overwatering vs. underwatering**

## Subhead
*The signs look identical — until you check the soil.*

## Hero
Two side-by-side leaves or plants showing the contrast: one over (limp, soggy soil visible), one under (crispy edges, dry cracked soil). Or a single illustration of a finger pressed into soil — the diagnostic gesture. Compositionally, two halves split vertically work well at the 2:3 ratio.

## Body — Two contrasting blocks

**OVERWATERING signs**
- Wilting in saturated soil
- Uniform yellowing
- Mushy stem, sour soil smell
- Fungus gnats at the surface

**UNDERWATERING signs**
- Wilting that recovers within an hour of watering
- Crispy brown leaf edges
- Soil pulled away from pot edges
- Plant lighter than usual to lift

## Callout (terracotta accent)

> First check: stick a finger 1–2 inches into the soil. Wet, cool, heavy = don't water. Dry, dusty = water.
