## Body — Problem bullets (9 numbered items, bold problem + arrow + short cause)

- **01 Yellow lower leaves** → water or nitrogen
- **02 Curling tops in summer** → heat stress or herbicide drift
- **03 Brown spots, yellow halos** → septoria leaf spot
- **04 No fruit despite flowers** → cool nights below 55°F (PNW)
- **05 Sunken black on fruit bottom** → blossom end rot, inconsistent watering
- **06 Cracked fruit** → irregular watering after dry stretch
- **07 Large green caterpillars** → tomato hornworms — hand-pick
- **08 Whitish papery patches on fruit** → sunscald from over-pruning
- **09 Wilting that doesn't recover** → fusarium or verticillium wilt

## Callout (terracotta accent)

> Blossom end rot is a calcium *availability* problem (water swings), not a calcium deficiency. Adding calcium won't help if watering is erratic.
