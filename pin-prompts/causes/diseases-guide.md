## The 12 guide panels
1. Powdery mildew → White, dust-like coating on leaves; easy to wipe off
2. Septoria leaf spot → Small brown circles with yellow halos and tan centers
3. Early blight → Brown spots with concentric bullseye rings, often with yellowing
4. Late blight → Large irregular dark patches on leaves, often dark brown to black with fuzzy or oily appearance
5. Damping off / pythium → Seedling collapse at the soil line, often overnight
6. Rust → Orange-brown spots on undersides of leaves, particularly beans, hollyhocks, garlic, sunflowers
7. Fusarium and verticillium wilt → Soil-borne fungal infections that block plant vascular system
8. Bacterial spot → Small dark spots that look greasy or water-soaked, often with yellow halos
9. Bacterial wilt → Sudden wilting of an entire plant, especially squash or cucumber
10. Crown gall → Rough swollen growths on stems near the soil line
11. Mosaic viruses → Mottled yellow-and-green leaves, distorted growth, stunting
12. Curly top, leaf roll viruses → Distorted leaves with curling and yellowing
