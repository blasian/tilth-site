## The 7 guide panels
1. Nitrogen → Pale, uniform yellowing of older leaves first; the plant is reabsorbing N for new growth
2. Phosphorus → Purplish or reddish older leaves, especially in cool spring soils
3. Potassium → Yellow or brown crispy leaf margins on older leaves; fruit may be small or misshapen
4. Calcium → Distorted new growth; blossom end rot on tomatoes, peppers, squash
5. Magnesium → Interveinal yellowing on older leaves — leaf veins stay green while tissue between yellows
6. Iron → Interveinal yellowing on the newest leaves — opposite of magnesium
7. pH lockout → Sometimes the nutrients are there but unavailable because soil pH is wrong
