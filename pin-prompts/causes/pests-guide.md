## The 14 guide panels
1. Slugs and snails → Irregular holes anywhere on the plant
2. Caterpillars → Cabbage worms / loopers — green caterpillars on brassicas
3. Flea beetles → Small dark beetles that jump like fleas when disturbed
4. Cucumber beetles → Striped or spotted yellow beetles on squash, cucumber
5. Earwigs → Damage to flowers and ripening fruit; some leaf damage
6. Deer and rabbits → Clean diagonal cuts on stems
7. Aphids → Clusters of small soft insects on undersides of new growth
8. Spider mites → Fine webbing, tiny pinpricks on leaves
9. Whiteflies → Tiny white insects that fly up in clouds when leaves are disturbed
10. Squash vine borer → Causes sudden wilting of an entire squash plant
11. Codling moth → Small worm in apple/pear cores
12. Root maggots → White larvae in soil; eat brassica and onion roots
13. Grubs → White C-shaped beetle larvae in soil
14. Cutworms → Gray-brown caterpillars that cut seedlings off at the base overnight
