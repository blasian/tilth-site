## Symptom name

## Pin headline
Why Are My Plant's Leaves Yellow?

## Pin subhead
6 causes, one fast first check

## Diagnostic callout
First check: Bottom leaves first → nitrogen, water issues, or disease (early blight, septoria). Top leaves first → micronutrient deficiency (iron, manganese) or virus. Random scattered yellowing → pest or pathogen

## The 6 diagnosis panels
1. Nitrogen deficiency → Pale uniform yellowing starting from the oldest leaves and moving up
2. Overwatering → Yellow leaves combined with soggy soil and sometimes wilting despite the wet
3. Underwatering → Yellowing with crispy, dry edges and bone-dry soil
4. Natural senescence → Only the very oldest leaves yellow and drop
5. Disease → Yellowing accompanied by spots, blotches, or rings on the leaves
6. Nutrient lockout from pH → Yellow leaves with green veins
