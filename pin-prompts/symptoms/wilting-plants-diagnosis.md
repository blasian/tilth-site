# Plant Wilting — quick diagnosis (PNW)

## Symptom name
Plant Wilting

## Landing URL

Why Is My Plant Wilting?

## Pin subhead
6 causes, one fast first check

## Diagnostic callout
First check: Recovers fully by next morning without intervention → heat-induced midday wilt. Normal. Doesn't recover even after deep watering → roots or vascular system are. Dry → underwatering. Water deeply.

## The 6 diagnosis panels
1. Heat-induced midday wilt → Plant looks limp during the hottest part of the day, then fully recovers by evening
2. Root rot → Plant wilted in wet soil
3. Fusarium or verticillium wilt → Wilting that progresses, often starting on one side or one branch and spreading
4. Transplant shock → Recently transplanted plant wilting for 1–3 weeks after planting
5. Damping off / pythium → Seedlings collapse at the soil line, often overnight
6. Heat or sunscald → Young transplant wilts in full sun, doesn't recover well even with watering
