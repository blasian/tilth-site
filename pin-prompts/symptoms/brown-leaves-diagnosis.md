## Pin subhead
6 causes, one fast first check

## Diagnostic callout
First check: Edges only (margins crispy) → fertilizer burn, heat scorch, wind damage. Whole sections (large patches) → water stress, disease, frost damage. Acute (one event) → fertilizer, frost, heat wave, wind

## The 6 diagnosis panels
1. Fertilizer burn → Uniform crispy bleaching along the leaf margins, symmetric across the plant
2. Heat scorch → Crispy edges or patches on sun-facing leaves only, often after an unusually hot stretch
3. Underwatering / drought stress → Whole leaves brown and dry, plant noticeably wilted or with curled leaves
4. Fungal disease → Brown spots that grow over time, often with yellow halos or concentric rings
5. Frost damage → Limp, wet-looking brown leaves the morning after a freeze, then they dry to crispy
6. Wind damage → Edges shredded, dried, or browned on the windward side of the plant, especially after a storm
