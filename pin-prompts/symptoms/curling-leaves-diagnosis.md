## Symptom name

## Pin subhead

## Diagnostic callout
First check: Curling downward and inward → water or heat stress. Curling upward and twisted → herbicide drift or virus. Cupped with sticky residue → aphids

## The 6 diagnosis panels
1. Heat or water stress → Leaves curl downward and inward to reduce surface area exposed to sun and wind
2. Herbicide drift → Leaves curl upward and look twisted, cupped, or strappy
3. Viral infection → Curling combined with yellow mottling, distortion, or stunted growth
4. Aphids → Clusters of small soft insects on the undersides of new growth
5. Calcium deficiency → Newest leaves emerge distorted, sometimes blackened along the margins
6. Tomato leaf roll → Older leaves on tomatoes curl up and stay there
