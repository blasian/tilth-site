## Diagnostic callout
First check: Flowers form but drop without setting fruit → pollination, heat, or temperature. No flowers at all on a mature plant → too much nitrogen

## The 8 diagnosis panels
1. Too much nitrogen → Lots of leafy growth, dark green leaves, no flowers at all
2. Insufficient pollination → Flowers form, look healthy, then drop without setting fruit
3. Heat stress → Sustained temperatures above 90°F cause flower drop in tomatoes, peppers, and beans
4. Cool nights → Tomatoes specifically need night temperatures above 55°F to set fruit
5. Plant immaturity → Plant has never bloomed; looks healthy but is too young
6. Insufficient sun → Plant grows leaves but doesn't bloom
7. Wrong day length → Plant doesn't bloom because day length doesn't match its requirement
8. Pruning at the wrong time → Plant looks healthy but doesn't bloom this year
